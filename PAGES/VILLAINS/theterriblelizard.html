The Terrible Lizard: Boston's Cold-Blooded King

James Colvin discovered his mutation at age 14 when a schoolyard fight ended with his opponent in the hospital from an unknown toxin. His working-class Irish family in South Boston tried to hide his abilities, but local gangs took notice. By 16, he worked as an enforcer, his natural venom making him valuable in "sending messages."

He built his empire methodically over 15 years. Starting with protection rackets in South Boston, he expanded into gambling, smuggling, and construction fraud. His nickname came from his cold, calculating nature and the painful deaths his venom caused. Unlike flashier criminals, Colvin runs his operation like a business.

His territory now includes the Seaport District and parts of South Boston. He maintains legitimate businesses in construction and waste management while controlling much of Boston's drug trade. He avoids direct conflict with other powered individuals, preferring to hire normal criminals and use his abilities only when necessary.

Key Aspects:

  • Runs operations from a historic townhouse in South Boston
  • Maintains connections with police and politicians through blackmail
  • Controls several unions through intimidation and bribery
  • Has a network of informants throughout the city
  • Never forgives betrayal, known for brutal reprisals
  • Keeps detailed records of every deal and favor

His current focus is expanding into biotech protection rackets, extorting companies that cannot afford legitimate security. This brings him into conflict with both heroes and rival criminals looking to exploit Boston's tech sector.
