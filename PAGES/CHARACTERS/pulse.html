Pulse: Resonance in the Dark

Musical Foundations
In the historic halls of Boston's Symphony Hall, where music soared for over a century, Marcia Chen found her calling not in playing instruments but in mastering the very nature of sound itself. Born to Lisa Chen, a renowned piano instructor at the New England Conservatory, and David Chen, the first violin for the Boston Symphony Orchestra, Marcia developed an intimate understanding of acoustics from her earliest days.

Unlike her sister Grace, who followed their parents' path into performance at Berklee College of Music, Marcia was fascinated by the physics of sound. She spent countless hours in Symphony Hall's hidden spaces, studying how sound waves moved through the historic architecture, learning their patterns and possibilities.

The Innovation
By her late twenties, Marcia had earned a reputation as an acoustic innovator. Her experimental waveguide system promised to revolutionize sound reproduction, creating perfect acoustic environments in any space. Dr. Sarah Thompson, her mentor and head of acoustic research, supported her increasingly ambitious experiments, even as others warned of potential risks.

The project drew attention from various technical circles, including fellow engineer James Morrison, whose work on wave propagation would later influence his path to become Ironclad. They spent long nights discussing theoretical applications of wave physics, unaware they were both on paths toward extraordinary transformation.

The Resonance
The accident occurred during a late-night calibration session. Marcia noticed abnormal patterns in the waveguide readings—harmonics that shouldn't exist, resonances that defied known physics. A massive power surge triggered a cascading feedback loop as she rushed to shut down the system. The overlapping sound waves created a standing pattern that trapped her in a node of pure acoustic energy.

For three minutes and seventeen seconds, Marcia existed in a state between solid matter and pure sound. The experience should have killed her. Instead, it fundamentally altered her relationship with sonic energy. Her body became a living waveguide, capable of generating, controlling, and manipulating sound in ways that defied scientific explanation.

Silent Recovery
The three weeks following the accident tested Marcia's sanity. Confined to a specially constructed isolation room at Massachusetts General Hospital, she struggled to control her new abilities. Random sonic pulses shattered windows and disrupted electronic equipment. Only her boyfriend Aiden, protected by experimental sound-dampening technology he developed at MIT, could safely visit.

Dr. Thompson converted her acoustics lab into a testing facility, working tirelessly to understand her condition. She discovered that Marcia's abilities manifested strongest in the infrasound range—frequencies below human hearing that could affect physical objects. This breakthrough helped Marcia begin to master her powers.

A New Symphony
Now, Marcia channels her powers with a precision that matches her former technical expertise. She can generate sonic blasts capable of shattering steel, create protective barriers of resonating air, and "see" through sophisticated echolocation. Her understanding of acoustic physics, combined with her mutant abilities, makes her a formidable hero and valued member of the BAA.

Yet challenges remain. A constant low-level hum accompanies her every moment, intensifying with stress. Her hypersensitive hearing requires regular meditation to control, and she can no longer attend the concerts that once filled her life without specialized protection. The accident left a stark white streak in her otherwise dark hair, a visible reminder of her transformation.

The Symphony Hall still stands as her sanctuary, though she now visits after hours when the space is empty. In those quiet moments, surrounded by the perfect acoustics that shaped her early career, Marcia practices the delicate control her powers demand. She's no longer just an engineer studying sound—she has become a conductor of sonic energy, composing a new symphony of power.
